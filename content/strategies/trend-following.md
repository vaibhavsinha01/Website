## The Core Principle

Trend following is built on a single empirical truth: **prices trend more than they should by chance**.

The approach doesn't try to predict where the market will go. Instead, it *reacts* to what the market is already doing — joining a move in progress and staying with it until the evidence says it's over.

It's not glamorous. But it works.

---

## Identifying a Trend

A trend exists when:

- **Uptrend**: Series of higher highs and higher lows (HH/HL structure)
- **Downtrend**: Series of lower highs and lower lows (LH/LL structure)

Use **multiple timeframes** to confirm:

1. **Weekly/Daily** — determine the macro direction
2. **4H/1H** — find the trend on your trading timeframe
3. **15m/5m** — fine-tune your entry timing

Only take trades in the direction of all three.

---

## Entry Techniques

### Pullback Entry

The bread and butter of trend following.

1. Wait for price to pull back to a significant level (previous structure, moving average, or Fibonacci zone)
2. Look for a **reversal signal** (engulfing candle, pin bar, or break of pullback structure)
3. Enter in the direction of the trend

**Advantages**: Lower risk entries, better R:R, avoids chasing

### Continuation Breakout

When a trend is particularly strong, pullbacks are shallow and quick.

1. Identify a small consolidation within the trend
2. Enter on the break of that micro-range in the trend direction
3. Stop just beyond the opposite side of the micro-range

---

## Stop Loss Placement

In a trend, your stop serves two purposes:
1. Define maximum loss if wrong
2. Lock in profit as the trend develops

**Initial stop**: Just beyond the last swing point opposite your trade direction.

**Trailing stop options**:
- Below each successive higher low (uptrend)
- Below a moving average (e.g., 20 EMA) on the entry timeframe
- Using an ATR-based trail (e.g., 2× ATR from recent high/low)

---

## Riding the Trend

This is where most traders fail. They exit too early.

Tactics for staying in winners:

- **Scale out partially** at predefined targets (1:1, 1:2), move stop to breakeven, run the rest
- **Only exit on structure break** — not because you "feel" the move is over
- **Time-based review** — check weekly: is the trend structure still intact?

> The profit from one well-ridden trend can compensate for 5–10 small losses. Let winners run.

---

## Market Selection

Not all markets trend equally well. Prioritise:

| Asset Class | Notes |
|-------------|-------|
| Forex majors | USD, EUR pairs trend well on higher timeframes |
| Indices | Long-term upward bias; trend clearly |
| Commodities | Oil, gold can have multi-week trends |
| Individual stocks | Look for earnings-driven momentum |

Avoid highly mean-reverting instruments (certain volatility products, flat equities) for this strategy.

---

## Risk Management

- Risk **1% per trade** regardless of conviction
- Never increase position size mid-trend (pyramiding requires strict rules)
- If three consecutive trend trades fail, reduce size and review market conditions

---

## Key Metrics to Track

Track these across at least 30 trades before drawing conclusions:

- Win rate (expect 35–45% — that's normal and profitable with good R:R)
- Average winner vs average loser
- Longest losing streak (emotional resilience test)
- Profit factor (aim for >1.5)

---

## Summary

Trend following rewards patience above all else. The setup isn't complex — the execution is.

Commit to the process. Trade the same way every time. Let the market do the work.
