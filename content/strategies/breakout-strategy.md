## Overview

The breakout strategy is one of the most powerful and time-tested approaches in technical trading. The core premise is straightforward: markets alternate between periods of **consolidation** (low volatility, range-bound) and **expansion** (directional movement, high momentum).

Our job is to identify the consolidation and position ourselves for the expansion.

---

## Why Breakouts Work

Markets consolidate for a reason — large players are accumulating or distributing positions. When they finish, price moves sharply in the direction of the absorbed pressure.

This creates two key conditions in our favour:

1. **Clear invalidation level** — if the breakout fails, we know immediately
2. **Asymmetric reward** — a genuine breakout typically travels far from the entry point

---

## Setup Requirements

For a valid breakout setup, all four conditions must be present:

- **Defined range** — at least 8–15 candles of consolidation with clear boundaries
- **Volume contraction** — volume shrinks during the base, indicating a pause rather than reversal
- **Structural significance** — the level being broken is one the market has respected multiple times
- **Trend context** — trade breakouts in the direction of the higher timeframe trend

> Don't trade every break of a level. You're looking for the *high-conviction* setup where all pieces align.

---

## Entry Rules

**Aggressive Entry (on close)**

Wait for a candle to close above (long) or below (short) the range boundary.

- Entry: Close of the breakout candle
- Stop: Below the consolidation low (long) / above consolidation high (short)

**Conservative Entry (on retest)**

After the initial break, price often returns to test the broken level. This offers a higher probability entry with tighter risk.

- Entry: Bounce off the retested level
- Stop: Below the retest candle low

---

## Position Sizing

Use fixed fractional position sizing. Risk no more than **1–2% of total account** per trade.

```
Position Size = (Account Risk %) × Account Balance / Distance to Stop (in $)
```

Example:
- Account: $50,000
- Risk per trade: 1% = $500
- Stop distance: $2.50
- Position size: 200 shares

---

## Targets

Use a minimum **2:1 reward-to-risk ratio**. For strong momentum breakouts, aim for 3:1 or higher.

Projected targets:
1. **T1**: 1.5× the height of the consolidation range
2. **T2**: Next major structure level on the chart
3. **T3**: Trail stop for runners in a strong trend

---

## Common Mistakes

- **Chasing** — entering after the move has already extended; wait for retest or skip
- **Wrong timeframe** — trading breakouts on 1-minute charts with no higher-timeframe context
- **Ignoring volume** — low-volume breakouts fail more often; look for rising volume on the break
- **Moving stop too early** — give the trade room to develop before managing

The breakout strategy is simple — but simple doesn't mean easy. Mastery comes from patience, selectivity, and flawless execution of the same setup, over and over.
