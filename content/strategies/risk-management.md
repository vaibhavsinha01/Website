## Risk Management Is the Strategy

Most traders spend 90% of their time looking for better entries. The traders who consistently make money spend 90% of their time managing risk.

This isn't a support document for your trading strategy. **This is the strategy.**

---

## The Three Levels of Risk

### 1. Position-Level Risk

The amount you're willing to lose on a *single trade*.

**Rule**: Never risk more than **1–2%** of your total account on any single position.

```
Max Loss ($) = Account Balance × Risk % 
Position Size = Max Loss / Distance to Stop
```

If your account is $20,000 and you risk 1%:
- Max loss per trade = $200
- Stop distance = $1.00
- Position size = 200 units

This rule keeps any single loss from being catastrophic.

### 2. Daily Loss Limit

The maximum drawdown you'll accept in a single trading day before stopping.

**Rule**: Stop trading for the day if you lose **3–5%** of account balance.

This protects you from "revenge trading" — the emotional spiral that turns a bad day into a devastating one.

### 3. Drawdown Limit

The maximum total drawdown from your peak equity before you step back, review, and reduce size.

**Rule**: If drawdown exceeds **10–15%**, cut position size by 50% and do a full strategy review.

## The Risk-Reward Minimum

Never take a trade with less than **2:1 reward-to-risk**.

This means: if your stop is $200 away, your target must be at least $400 away.

Why this matters mathematically:

| Win Rate | R:R Ratio | Expected Value Per Trade |
|----------|-----------|--------------------------|
| 40% | 1:1 | -0.20 (losing) |
| 40% | 2:1 | +0.20 (winning) |
| 40% | 3:1 | +0.60 (winning well) |

A 40% win rate with 2:1 R:R is *profitable*. This is why R:R matters more than win rate.

## Correlation Risk

A common mistake: holding multiple positions that are highly correlated.

> If you're long EUR/USD, GBP/USD, and AUD/USD simultaneously, you don't have three separate trades. You have one large USD short trade.

**Rule**: Limit total exposure to correlated instruments. Treat correlated positions as a single larger position for risk purposes.

---

## The Psychology of Drawdown

Losing streaks are mathematically inevitable. With a 40% win rate, you will experience:

- 3 consecutive losses: probability ~21%
- 5 consecutive losses: probability ~8%
- 7 consecutive losses: probability ~3%

These *will happen*. The framework above ensures they don't end your trading career.

> "The goal of risk management is not to avoid all losses. It's to stay in the game long enough for your edge to compound."

---

## Practical Checklist

Before every trade:

- [ ] Have I defined my exact stop level?
- [ ] Is my position size within the 1–2% rule?
- [ ] Is my minimum target at least 2× my stop distance?
- [ ] Am I already at my daily loss limit?
- [ ] Does this position correlate with something I'm already holding?

If any answer is "no", don't take the trade.

---

## Summary

Risk management isn't restrictive — it's liberating. When you know your maximum loss on any trade is controlled, you can execute without fear.

The traders who last are the ones who protect capital first and grow it second.
